## Pre Hackathon
- Choose tracks that others aren't likely to pick.
	- An even better idea is to deviate from the problem statement a bit under the same track to pick something fresh. Teams often end up building the same thing so standing out among that helps a lot.
- Always work backwards, don't focus on the technical solution, focus on the judges
- Build something that judges are likely to find exciting
	- A good metric is to see if you yourself are excited, the best metric is when your entire team is excited
	- You guys need to be your own worst critics, harshly criticize ideas that don't make sense to you
- The **IDEA** makes or breaks a hackathon win. It is the single biggest factor, it doesn't matter how complex or cool your solution is if the core **IDEA** is unimpressive.
	- Youre **CORE** feature should be able to carry its own weight, this is my style
	- Another style that i've seen that works is **feature-bloat**
- Brainstorm the hell out of things, coding is no longer the bottleneck
## During Hackathon
- Have a hackathon doc ready
	- Research (pre)
	- Idea brainstorm (pre)
	- Feature brainstorm (pre)
	- Resources
	- TODOs
	- Demo plan/script
- Abuse the heck out of mentors present, ask them ruthlessly for reviews/feedback. A fresh set of ideas always helps and 9/10 the same mentors will be judging so if you fix all their complaints its a huge green-flag.
- Don't limit yourself to the problem statement or track, be as creative as you can. Understand what the judges want to see.
- Add some niche/buzzword tech like microservices/websockets. It helps give judges something to highlight from your team.
- Keep updating the doc with all of the ideas that your team comes up with and with mentor feedback.
## Demo
- DO NOT FUCK WITH THE CODE BEFORE A DEMO! Freeze everything atleast an hour before the demo, YOU DO NOT WANT TO DEMO UNTESTED CODE! If you really need to make sure you have a backup on another branch.
- Scalability is an easy point that judges seem to love for some reason
- Plan your demo, the goal is impact and storytelling. Use your best speaker, everyone does not need to speak.
	- Live demos are risky but worth it if they work out 
	- Always have a video as a backup
### Presentation Tips
1. Explain the background or motivation for the solution
2. FOCUS on the main point, make it well defined and concise, do **not beat around the bush**, the audience should grasp it quickly.
3. Explain USP (unique selling point)
4. Explain PMF( Product Market Fit ). I.e. how well your product caters to the market demand
5. Scalability
6. If you have a lot of features, make a PPT. It will help with flow and prevent you from going offtrack
